e-Krishok: Farmer’s Knowledge Network

Farmers lack in access to relevant and timely information and knowledge about new technologies, skills, practices and ways to collaborate with market intermediaries and resources to overcome these limitations. As a result, they are vulnerable to crop related diseases, suffer productivity constraints and fail to take advantage of potential farming opportunities.

Through e-Krisshok, BIID will facilitate farmers’ access to information and advisory services to help maximize farm productivity and economic gain. The initiative branded as ‘e-Krishok’ , has been launched in collaboration with Grameen Phone’s Community Information Center Project with the aim of empowering farmers through participatory networks managed by them and facilitated by respective CIC operators and market intermediaries to access, generate, share and utilize information and knowledge for increased productivity and improved livelihoods. BIID has already initiated the pilot phase of ‘e-Krishok’ to harness collective awareness, voice and participation of farmers in 12 GP - CIC coverage areas to develop, promote and deliver CIC based information and advisory services and to facilitate transfer of skills, knowledge & technology to help farmers address the most critical constraints in realizing desired yield.
