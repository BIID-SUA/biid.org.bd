BIID is working with Katalyst, Bangladesh, a project funded by consortium of donors mandated to contribute to poverty reduction through increased enterprise competitiveness – BIID is engaged in:

î Supporting Grameen Phone (GP) to roll out community information centers (GP-CICs) across the country. Specific services offered by BIID include location and entrepreneur profiling, training and development of HR capacity of the GP-CICs, community outreach/awareness campaigns, services development (existing and new).

 î Collaborating with different public and private entities to develop an information platform for dissemination of IT enabled agricultural content

 î Facilitating access to ICT based public services and information for farmers, entrepreneurs and rural community through public-private partnership as a component of piloting e-Governance at local level.

 î Facilitating farmers’ access to information and advisory services to help maximize farm productivity and economic gain by farmers. The initiative branded as ‘e-Krishok’ , has been launched in collaboration with Grameen Phone’s Community Information Center Project with the aim of empowering farmers through participatory networks managed by them and facilitated by respective CIC operators and market intermediaries to access, generate, share and utilize information and knowledge for increased productivity and improved livelihoods. BIID has already initiated the pilot phase of ‘e-Krishok’ to harness collective awareness, voice and participation of farmers in 12 GP - CIC coverage areas to develop, promote and deliver CIC based information and advisory services and to facilitate transfer of skills, knowledge & technology to help farmers address the most critical constraints in realizing desired yield.
